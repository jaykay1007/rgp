Our Offset Printing Process

1

Design & File Preparation

We begin with your design files, ensuring they meet our printing specifications. Our prepress team checks color modes, resolution, bleed, and other critical elements.

2

Proofing

We provide digital proofs for your approval before printing begins. This ensures colors, layout, and content are exactly as you intended.

3

Plate Making

Once approved, we create printing plates for each color in your design. These aluminum plates transfer ink to the rubber blanket in the printing process.

4

Press Setup

Our skilled press operators set up the offset press with your specific paper, ink colors, and printing requirements.

5

Printing

The offset printing begins, with continuous quality checks throughout the run to ensure consistent color and detail reproduction.

6

Finishing

After printing, your materials undergo finishing processes like cutting, folding, binding, laminating, or specialty finishing as required.

7

Quality Control

Every printed piece undergoes rigorous quality inspection before packaging to ensure it meets our high standards.

8

Delivery

Your completed printing project is carefully packaged and delivered to your specified location on time.

Frequently Asked Questions About Offset Printing

What is the difference between offset and digital printing?

Offset printing uses plates and rubber blankets to transfer ink to paper, while digital printing applies toner or ink directly to the paper without plates. Offset printing offers superior quality and cost-effectiveness for larger quantities, while digital printing is better for smaller runs and variable data printing.

What minimum quantity do you recommend for offset printing?

Offset printing becomes cost-effective typically at quantities of 500 or more pieces due to the setup and plate-making costs. For smaller quantities, digital printing may be more economical.

What file formats do you accept for offset printing?

We accept print-ready PDF files with embedded fonts, CMYK color mode, and proper bleed settings. We can also work with Adobe Illustrator (.ai), InDesign (.indd), and Photoshop (.psd) files with all linked images and fonts.

How long does offset printing take?

Standard offset printing jobs typically take 3-7 business days from approval to delivery, depending on complexity, quantity, and finishing requirements. Rush services are available for urgent projects.

Do you offer design services for offset printing projects?

Yes, our skilled graphic designers can create custom designs for any offset printing project, from business stationery to catalogs and packaging. Design services are charged separately from printing.

What paper options are available for offset printing?

We offer a wide range of paper options including different weights, finishes (gloss, matte, uncoated), textures, and specialty papers. Our team can help you select the perfect paper for your specific project needs.
